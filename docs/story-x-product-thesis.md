# Story X Product Thesis

For the current product requirements document, see [`docs/story-x-prd.md`](story-x-prd.md).

Story X is a story-first creative operating system.

Most AI creation tools start from an output request: write a chapter, draw an image, make a video, summarize an idea. Story X starts one layer earlier. It asks whether the story is strong enough to survive the output.

## Core Belief

The most important product is not a paragraph, panel, voice file, or video cut. The most important product is a story that can keep its promise.

An essay can be intimate or commercial. A novel can be read alone or sold. A comic can stay still or later become motion. In every case, the value comes from story quality:

- What is the promise?
- Why does it matter?
- Who changes?
- What must not collapse?
- What should the audience remember?

## Three AI Resources

Story X treats AI capabilities as three production resources.

| Resource | Used For | Risk If Unharnessed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Text | story, prose, essay, dialogue, lyrics, script, captions | fluent but hollow writing |
| Image | character, panel, storyboard, visual motif, cover, frame | pretty but inconsistent visuals |
| Sound | narration, voice tone, music cue, rhythm, effects | polished but emotionally flat audio |

The product is the harness that combines them.

## Harness Engineering

Harness engineering means the system does not simply call an AI model and accept the result. It sets context, roles, gates, memory, checks, and transformation rules.

Story X harnesses:

- story ontology
- character contracts
- world rules
- author voice bible
- audio rhythm plans
- visual continuity
- media transformation paths
- export and publishing decisions

The AI resources are powerful, but the harness decides what they are allowed to change.

## Media Transformation

Story X should make it easy to move from one medium to another without starting over.

Examples:

- Novel to audiobook
- Novel to screenplay
- Novel to webtoon board
- Essay to published post
- Essay to narrated audio
- Essay to video essay
- Insta-toon to cut-by-cut video
- Comic board to motion sequence
- Children's story to song reading

Each transformation must preserve:

- story promise
- character or narrator voice
- core emotion
- canon facts
- audience expectation

## Product Promise

Story X helps creators break the old production pipeline.

You do not need to decide at the beginning whether an idea is only a novel, essay, comic, or audiobook. You can first make the story strong, then let the system suggest the next useful form.

The output can be read, watched, listened to, sold, posted, or expanded. The center remains the same:

**The story has to be good.**
